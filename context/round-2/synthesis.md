## Prose digest

**What changed between rounds:** Across the board, scope cuts and concrete-detail pivots turned into score gains. p1 jumped +1.5 (7.5 → 9.0) as the four pivots from r1 (TS Playground homepage, AI SDK before/after, hard non-template-logic scope line, pluggable delimiter, `.validate()` Result type) closed every concern Matt or Theo raised, and Rauch's "primitive needs an integration story" objection was satisfied by the AI SDK demo being the headline. p3 jumped +1.75 (5.75 → 7.5) — the largest movement of any proposal — entirely because cutting the hosted UI / Supabase / Lambda / Stripe stack, plus reframing the registry as a static manifest with a lockfile and human-rewritten error messages, dissolved the "this is Langfuse with a codegen step" objection that nearly killed it in r1. p2 climbed only +0.5 (6.0 → 6.5) because Rauch held his structural dissent and the cross-persona "instruction-block edits is underspecified" concern persisted from r1 into r2.

**What's converging:** All four personas now agree p1 is shippable as-is and is the foundational bet. Three of four (Matt, Theo, Addy) agree on the OSS-CLI-first sequencing for p2 and p3. The lockfile concept for p3 emerged independently from three personas — strong signal it's the right architecture. The "make the error messages the product" framing for p3, originating from Rauch, was implicitly endorsed by Theo's "the launch story should lean into the CI failure screenshot, not the API" — convergent on the user-facing surface. The static-manifest-over-public-bucket choice is uncontested.

**What's still contested:** Exactly one structural dissent remains: **Rauch on p2's launch architecture**. He believes OSS-CLI-only is the wrong shape — that the hosted GitHub App with a free tier is the product, that OSS should be the diff algorithm only, and that shipping the App later doubles the migration cost. Matt and Theo explicitly opposed the hosted layer in v1, so this is a non-contradictory-pivot conflict, not a synthesis ambiguity — we held the 3-of-4 majority and preserved Rauch's position verbatim for the user. A secondary open question, raised by Addy on p3, is empirical and can only be resolved post-launch: do PMs actually edit raw JSON manifests, or does the no-redeploy pitch require a UI to land? Both are flagged for round 3 / final-mode consideration.
